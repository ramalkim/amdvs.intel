ABOUT PROCESSORS




INTRODUCTION

A processor is a little chip residing on computers and other electronic devices ("microprocessor,"). Its fundamental role is to collect feedback and produce the correct output. This may seem like a simple job, but modern processors can handle trillions per second of calculations. Multiple processors, which work together to process instructions, are also part of modern CPUs. When these two processors are comprised in one psychological entitlement, they are both mileages; In reality, you can see different graphs on each processor if you display the output of your device using a system monitoring tool such as Windows or the Activity Monitor (Mac OS X). Processors with two cores are referred to as dual-core processors, while those with four cores are referred to as quad-core processors. Some high-end workstations have multiple CPUs with multiple cores which allow for eight, 12 and even more processing cores for one single machine.
